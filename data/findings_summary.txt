## ğŸ”¹ Analyst Findings (SOC / Vulnerability Analyst View)

### 1. Discovery & Enumeration

Active scanning was conducted using Nmap with service detection and vulnerability enumeration enabled.

**Key activities performed:**

* Service discovery using `-sV`
* Vulnerability enumeration using `--script vuln`
* Artifact capture using `-oN` for audit-ready evidence

**Notable services discovered:**

* OpenSSH (TCP/22)
* Splunkd Management & Web Interfaces (TCP/8000, 8089)
* HTTP services susceptible to Slowloris DoS (CVE-2007-6750)

Exploit references and CVE mappings were identified via Vulners integration, providing immediate threat context.

---

### 2. Asset Context Enrichment

Raw scan data was enriched with asset inventory metadata to support risk-based decision-making.

**Contextual attributes applied:**

* Business unit ownership
* Asset criticality
* Data sensitivity classification
* Network exposure (internet-facing vs internal)

This enrichment enabled differentiation between **technical severity** and **true organizational risk**, preventing over-escalation of low-impact findings.

---

### 3. Risk-Based Triage & Prioritization

Findings were triaged using a combination of:

* Vulnerability severity
* Asset criticality
* Exposure level
* Potential business impact

**High-risk findings prioritized:**

* Internet-facing services on high-criticality assets
* Services with known exploit references and availability impact

**Deprioritized / Accepted Risk:**

* Slowloris DoS identified on localhost-only services
  *Rationale:* Non-production, lab-scoped exposure with no external attack surface.

This reflects standard SOC and vulnerability governance workflows, where **context determines actionability**.

---

### 4. Analyst Judgment & Decision-Making

Rather than treating all vulnerabilities equally, analyst judgment was applied to:

* Identify findings requiring escalation
* Document accepted risk with justification
* Preserve evidence for audit and review

This demonstrates maturity beyond tool execution and aligns with how senior analysts operate in enterprise environments.
